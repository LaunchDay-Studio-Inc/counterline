## Email 2 — Plans After the Exit (Day 2)

**Subject:** What to play after move 10

The memorised line gets you to the exit position. What matters is what you do next.

**As White (Vienna Gambit):**
After the queen exchange (Qxe7+), your three priorities are:
1. Centralise the king via Kd2
2. Play Ng5 targeting f7
3. Activate your rooks on the open b- and f-files

**As Black (Caro-Kann):**
After reaching the fortress, your three priorities are:
1. Complete development: ...Be7, ...O-O, ...Ngf6
2. Maintain the pawn chain: keep pawns on c6, d5, e6
3. Wait for White to overextend — your position is solid; time is on your side

Chapter 6 (Key Plans and Patterns) covers these in detail with diagrams.

## Email 3 — Deviations (Day 3)

**Subject:** When they don't follow the script

Your opponent hasn't read this book. They might deviate.

**Good news:** deviations almost always favour you.

When someone deviates from the Vienna Gambit mainline (e.g., 3...d5 instead of 3...exf4), they are entering a position where you still have a solid setup and they've voluntarily avoided the strongest continuation. The book covers the five most common deviations for each line.

**Key principle:** When the opponent deviates, CounterLine's specialist has no opinion — you're playing normal Stockfish. And normal Stockfish is the strongest chess engine in the world.

Chapter 7 has the full deviation guide.

## Email 4 — Drills (Day 5)

**Subject:** Lock the lines into memory

Knowing the moves is not the same as *remembering* them under pressure.

The book includes three types of drills:

1. **Seed Line Memory** — fill-in-the-blank quizzes for both lines
2. **Position Evaluation** — given a position from the repertoire, assess who stands better and why
3. **Deviation Response** — opponent plays a sideline; what's your response?

Spend 15 minutes on these before your next rated game. The difference between "I think it's Nc3" and "I know it's Nc3" is the difference between confidence and anxiety in the opening.

Chapter 8 has all the drills with answers.

**Subject:** What we tested. What we didn't.

One more thing before you go.

CounterLine does not claim to be stronger than Stockfish. It claims to have scored 52.50% against Stockfish 18 on a published fixed opening suite. That's a positive trend from 40-game matches — meaningful, but not yet proven at the 95% confidence level that statisticians use as a threshold.

Why tell you this? Because transparency is the product. Every PGN file is published. Every reproduction command is in the book. You can run the matches yourself and see if you get the same results.

Chapter 10 covers the exact boundaries of what has been tested and what has not. Read it. It's the most important chapter in the book for anyone who values evidence.
